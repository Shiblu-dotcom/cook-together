# Cook Together, Stay Together — Full Concept Breakdown

*A research and positioning document. Last updated July 2026.*

---

## 1. The one-liner

**A 15-minute cooking game that tricks couples into having a real conversation.**

Longer: Cook Together is a relationship experience disguised as a cooking
challenge. Two people, one kitchen, secret ingredients, surprise questions,
and an AI judge that knows how their day went. Every night ends with one
word — chosen by the AI to capture the couple's energy — saved forever to
their collection.

## 2. The problem

- **The date-night rut.** Long-term couples default to "dinner and a show" —
  passive, expensive, and interchangeable. Novelty is what keeps
  relationships alive (see: self-expansion theory, Aron et al.), and novelty
  is exactly what routine kills.
- **Together but separate.** Couples spend evenings in the same room on
  different screens. Most "couples apps" make this worse — each partner
  quietly answering quizzes on their own phone.
- **Conversation atrophy.** "How was your day?" → "Fine." The questions that
  actually connect people ("What's something you've been meaning to tell
  me?") feel forced without a container that gives permission to ask them.
- **Date nights cost money.** A restaurant date is $60–150. Groceries for a
  cook-off are already in the fridge.

## 3. The solution — the core loop

One "night" takes ~25 minutes end to end:

1. **Check-in** — each partner privately tells the app about their day
   (typed or dictated). This is the secret sauce: the AI now knows context
   no other game has.
2. **Theme + secret ingredients** — the AI picks a cooking theme fitting
   their mood; each player privately receives a theme-matched secret
   ingredient the other must not learn.
3. **The cook-off** — 15 minutes, one kitchen, ambient music, a mid-cook
   "twist" (swap stations! one hand only!), question cards that slide up
   and collect private answers, a voice-activated AI Chef for help, and a
   camera for capturing memories (photos = points).
4. **Judgment** — an AI judge with a personality chosen for tonight's mood
   scores both dishes (it *sees* the photos), reacts to each plate
   personally, references their day, and names their couple title.
5. **The reveal** — the private question answers are shown to each other
   for the first time. This is the intimacy payload.
6. **The Word** — one word, chosen by the AI, that captures tonight.
   Saved to a permanent collection. Over months, the collection becomes an
   emotional map of the relationship.
7. **The keepsake** — a shareable result card (the viral loop) and a
   growing profile: badges, compatibility history, memory wall, story card.

## 4. What makes it unlike anything else

| # | Differentiator | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The AI knows your day before judging your dish** | Personalization no cooking app or party game has — the judge says "after the day you had, this dish is a victory" |
| 2 | **The Word** | A one-word emotional artifact per night; collection = retention mechanic AND emotional moat |
| 3 | **One phone, one room** | Deliberately anti-screen-isolation; pass-the-phone privacy etiquette is part of the fun |
| 4 | **Questions hidden until the reveal** | Creates a delayed-intimacy moment neither person can rush |
| 5 | **Memory earns points** | Taking photos is strategy, not vanity — the game manufactures its own keepsakes |
| 6 | **Judge personality adapts** | Savage after a good day, gentle after a hard one — same app, different night every time |
| 7 | **Vision judging** | The AI reacts to the actual plate, not just its description |

## 5. Target audience

**Primary:** cohabiting couples 24–40, together 1+ years, comfortable with
apps, feeling the routine set in. They cook at home at least sometimes.

**Personas:**
- *The re-sparkers* — 30s, together 5+ years, actively looking for
  date-night ideas (they follow date-idea TikTok accounts).
- *The new-nesters* — recently moved in together, building rituals.
- *Long-distance graduates* — finally in one place, hungry for shared
  activities.

**Secondary:** friends/roommates (competitive mode), families (parent+teen
cook-offs), gift market (anniversary/Valentine's "date night in a link").

## 6. Market context — what to research

The app straddles four categories. Research each:

**a) Couples apps** (direct-ish competitors)
- *Paired* (~$9/mo, daily questions, millions of downloads) — research its
  retention claims and App Store reviews for complaints ("feels like
  homework" is common).
- *Agapé, Lasting, Evergreen, Love Nudge* — question/quiz format.
- **Gap they all share:** partners use them separately, passively. None
  create a shared real-world activity.

**b) Date-night subscription boxes**
- *DateBox Club, Happily, Crated with Love* ($30–40/box) — physical proof
  that couples pay monthly for structured date nights.
- **Research:** their churn rates, review themes, whether "cooking date"
  boxes are their best sellers.

**c) Cooking apps/experiences**
- *SideChef, Kitchen Stories* (recipes, not games), *Airbnb Experiences*
  cooking classes ($50–120/head — validates willingness to pay for cooking
  as a date).
- **Gap:** nothing gamifies cooking *against/with* your partner.

**d) Party/social games**
- *Jackbox* model (one screen, everyone plays) proves the one-device party
  loop. No couples-intimacy title exists in that catalog.

**Search terms for research:** "couples app market size", "Paired app
revenue", "date night statistics couples", "relationship app retention",
"cooking date night trend TikTok", "#datenightideas volume", "self
expansion theory couples novelty".

**Communities to listen in:** r/relationships (date rut threads),
r/Cooking, date-idea TikTok/Instagram accounts (check follower counts —
this is the marketing channel), couples-therapy adjacent podcasts.

## 7. Monetization options (research which fits)

| Model | Reference | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Freemium subscription | Paired ($9/mo) | Free: 2 nights/mo. Paid: unlimited nights, all judge personalities, premium voices, story exports |
| One-time purchase | Jackbox ($25–30) | Fits "game" framing; weaker recurring revenue |
| Consumable packs | — | Themed night packs: Anniversary, Valentine's, "Meet the Parents Prep" |
| Gift links | DateBox | "Buy a night for a couple" — wedding/anniversary gift channel |
| B2B | — | Corporate couples events, apartment-community events, brand-sponsored ingredient themes |

**Unit economics note:** each AI night costs roughly $0.05–0.15 in API
calls (judgment + context + chat) — very healthy against a $9/mo
subscription.

## 8. Growth loops built into the product

1. **The result card** — every night produces a designed, shareable image
   with both names and "The Word." Couples posting these = organic
   acquisition. (Research: Wordle's share-card-driven growth.)
2. **The word collection** — sunk emotional cost; switching apps means
   abandoning your relationship's map. Retention moat.
3. **Badges/milestones** — 1st, 3rd, 7th, 15th night nudge streak behavior
   without daily-streak pressure.
4. **Gifting** — a night is inherently giftable ("we loved it — sent it to
   my sister and her husband").

## 10. Validation plan — the questions research must answer

1. **Does the pass-the-phone etiquette hold?** (Or do couples demand two
   phones?) → watch 5 couples play; count "accidental secret leaks."
2. **Is 15 minutes right?** → observed completion vs. abandonment mid-cook.
3. **Do people return?** → % of couples who play night 2 within 2 weeks
   (the single most important metric).
4. **Does the reveal land?** → observe: do couples talk about the answers
   after the game ends? That after-conversation is the real product.
5. **Will they pay?** → fake-door test a "$6.99/mo unlimited nights" screen.
6. **Is the AI judge the draw or the wrapper?** → A/B canned vs. real AI
   judgment on perceived quality.

## 11. Risks & open questions

- **Novelty cliff:** is this a 3-night wonder? (Counter: AI-generated
  questions/themes make content infinite; word collection compounds.)
- **Kitchen friction:** requires groceries, energy, a cleanable kitchen —
  higher activation cost than a quiz app.
- **Two-player requirement:** unusable solo; every session needs both
  partners bought in.
- **AI cost & safety:** hostile inputs to a judge that "roasts" could
  produce hurtful output — needs tone guardrails (partially addressed via
  judge-personality prompt).
- **Privacy:** day check-ins are intimate data. Currently local-only —
  a genuine marketing point ("your check-ins never leave your phone" on
  the static build).

## 12. Roadmap sketch

- **Now:** activate real AI (Vercel proxy — built, needs account), test
  with 3–5 real couples, fix what they break.
- **Next:** AI-generated questions/twists from couple history (infinite
  content), anniversary recaps, premium neural voices (ElevenLabs via
  proxy), localization.
- **Later:** two-device realtime mode (Supabase), themed packs, gift
  links, iOS/Android store wrappers (Capacitor), B2B events.
